Childhood Years

Shanizza Harvey loves to read books from the very beginning.1 In her childhood years, around ten or eleven years old, she was already reading books of her sisters which are usually stories from Wattpad. Stories like Diary ng Panget, Teen Clash, and She’s Dating The Gangster; the kind of stories that feels cringe reading today but feels like home every time she stumbles into it.2 She also likes to watch tagalog animes on TV in peaceful mornings. As a child, she wanted to be an astronaut or a ballerina because she was inspired by cartoons she’s watched.3 Apart from that, she also likes to go outside and do absolutely everything; playing hide and seek and tumbang preso, be a mother on chinese garter, keeping stones that are small and smooth, collecting pogs and texts.4 At home, oh, she doesn’t like to be at home, like she thinks when going to school.5 Her mother is a bit strict and her sisters are much old than her.6 She’s the seventh of her sisters, you see.7 The sister before her has a nine years age gap between them.8 The only savior in their house is probably her Dad because he loves to spoil her that much and calls her ‘his favorite and beautiful daughter’.9 Her father even went with her to the mall, Ever, which is located in Quezon City.10 She loves that mall very much because of her father.11 She also loves to go to TomsWorld there and try all those arcade games12 And some other times, they will go to Puregold around Quezon City too, and buy pizza.13 She adores it every time her father praises her that she ate almost all the pizza, which is why she tries to eat everything whenever they go there.14 Like a typical child, she discovers she has fears too; she fears heights, falling off the stairs, spiders, and cockroaches.15 She had experienced falling from the stairs and her dad treated her Cornetto to calm her down.16 Honestly, if she could relive one memory in her childhood days, it’s most likely where she had fun with her Dad and his one box of pizza.17 It really was my most fond memories of her childhood.18 She’s a Daddy’s girl, after all.19 She thinks, her childhood is probably one of her most colorful days.20
